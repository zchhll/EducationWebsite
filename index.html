About Me

Here's a timeline of my life.

  • 2003

    Born in Provo, Utah

    During my early childhood years, I was a small, chubby-cheeked kid who loved nothing more than playing outside with my siblings. As the third child, I looked up to my two older siblings and followed their lead in games and adventures. My parents were amazing, always making life fun and full of love. We lived in a cozy duplex at first and later moved to a house with a red door, which became the backdrop for many happy memories.

  • 2008 - 2012

    Raised in Sydney, Australia

    From ages 4 to 8, I lived in Sydney, Australia, where I learned to swim, ride a bike, and do flips on the trampoline. I loved living near the beach and traveling to many countries around Australia, which made the experience unforgettable. However, not everything was perfect—I didn’t enjoy the huge bugs and animals, and we were robbed twice, which was scary. Despite that, those years were filled with growth, adventure, and new experiences.

  • 2013 - 2020

    Participated in Numerous Sports

    From ages 8 to 18, I was deeply involved in sports, excelling as a competitive gymnast and even winning state. I also played football, lacrosse, basketball, and tennis, and I learned how to ski and snowboard. One of my favorite experiences was playing tennis with my close friend Tyler at Provo High School, where we competed as doubles partners in the state tournament. Adding to the fun, our dads were also doubles partners, making it a unique and special connection for both of our families.

  • 2021 - Present

    Continuing My Education

    I'm a student at Brigham Young University. I started with a summer and fall semester right after high school before serving a two-year mission. Since returning, I've been navigating potential career paths, with a particular interest in accounting. I enjoy learning and am excited about the opportunities and growth college has to offer.

Fall 2024

Here's a brief overview of my fall semester classes.

In Foundations of the Restoration, I learned about the origins, teachings, and impact of the Restoration of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. In Global Women's Studies, I studied the roles, rights, and experiences of women across cultures and history. Accounting taught me foundational principles of financial recordkeeping, reporting, and analysis. Beginning Writing and Rhetoric helped me develop skills in persuasive writing, argumentation, and effective communication. In Information Systems 201, I gained insights into managing data, technology, and systems to solve organizational challenges.

  • Teachers: Rob Eaton, Ramona Cutri, Erin Rhees, Melissa Larson, Laura Cutler
  • Sections: REL C 225, GWS 222, WRTG 150, ACC 200, IS 201
